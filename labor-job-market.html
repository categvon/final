LABOR & JOB MARKET

Trends, Outlook & Structural Conditions

The fields of librarianship and archival work are growing, but slowly. The Bureau of Labor Statistics reports a 2% increase for library and library media specialists and a 6% increase for archivists during the 2023–2024 year. Factors such as upcoming retirements, the emergence of roles in the digital space, and increased programming contribute to this growth.

Job classifications are an area that MLIS students should be aware of when entering the field. Though there are formal hiring constraints, public librarianship is classified as a municipal job or “civil service,” meaning employees are hired by local government and may be represented by the American Federation of State, County, and Municipal Employees (AFSCME Local 2626). Being part of a union affords protections for workplace rights, negotiated wages, and benefits such as pensions and healthcare.

Archivists, on the other hand, are typically hired on a contractual basis and often for wages lower than those of public librarians. For this to change, archivists would need to be included within civil service classifications, an effort that would likely require advocacy from senior leadership, such as library directors and senior librarians.
