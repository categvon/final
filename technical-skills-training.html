TECHNICAL SKILLS & TRAINING

Entry-Level Expectations & Preparation

This technical skill snapshot highlights the kinds of skills needed for entry-level roles in public-facing special collections, where everyday technical decisions shape how collections are cared for and used. It reflects work that supports access behind the scenes—including processing and description, digitization, metadata creation, and visitor services—and shows how this labor affects how people encounter collections. The snapshot also points to emerging areas such as digital asset management, introductory coding, and community-centered archival practices.

Technical Skills

Collections Care

Entry-level workers support inventory, care, and basic management of collection materials.

Digitization

Understanding how digitization and item selection support access, even when not leading projects.

Visitor Services

Technical work is closely tied to patron access and public service.

Archival Processing

Familiarity with finding aids and archival description supports access to archival collections.

Metadata

Descriptive information enables discovery and use of collections.

Skills Identified As Growing in Importance

Digital Asset Management Systems (DAMS)

Growing expectations for familiarity with DAMS and digital collections workflows.

Coding & Scripting (e.g., Python)

Increasingly common in training, though not universally required.

Community Archives Practices

Skills that support community-based collections are an area of growth.

Professional Job Listings & Preferred Skills

Examples from Current Postings

Accessioning Archivist — UCLA Library Special Collections

This role focuses on accessioning and stewardship of physical and digital archival collections.

  • Accession archival collections, including new acquisitions and backlogs
  • Maintain accession records and documentation
  • Support stewardship of physical and digital archival materials
  • Collaborate with library and collections staff

Special Collections Archivist Librarian — Denver Public Library

This role includes arranging and describing archival materials in multiple formats, supporting preservation workflows, and assisting with reference and outreach.

  • Arrange and describe archival materials in multiple formats
  • Support collections management and preservation activities
  • Provide reference, access, and outreach services
  • Apply archival standards within a public library setting
