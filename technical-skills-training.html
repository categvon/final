TECHNICAL SKILLS & TRAINING

Entry-Level Expectations & Preparation

This technical skill snapshot highlights the kinds of skills needed for entry-level roles in public-facing special collections, where everyday technical decisions shape how collections are cared for and used. It reflects work that supports access behind the scenes, including processing and description, digitization, metadata creation, and visitor services, while also showing how this labor shapes how people encounter collections. The snapshot also points to emerging areas such as digital asset management, introductory coding, and community-centered archival practices.

Technical Skills

Collections Care

Entry-level workers support inventory, care, and basic management of collection materials.

Digitization

Knowledge of how materials are digitized to support access and use of collections.

Visitor Services

Technical work is closely tied to patron access and public service.

Archival Processing

Familiarity with finding aids and archival description supports access to archival collections.

Metadata

Descriptive information enables discovery and use of collections.

Digital Asset Management

Awareness of systems used to manage digital collections, including repositories that support organization, storage, preservation, and access to digital assets.

Coding and Data Skills

Basic familiarity with coding languages such as Python can support tasks such as, data cleanup, metadata work, and managing digital collections.

Professional Development

Technical skills are often developed through training, workshops, and professional conferences, allowing staff to build knowledge in areas such as digital preservation and digital collections management.

Professional Job Listings & Preferred Skills

These listings offer real examples of technical skills expected in entry-level special collections work.

Accessioning Archivist: UCLA Library Special Collections

This role focuses on accessioning and stewardship of physical and digital archival collections.

Responsibilities include:

  • Accession archival collections, including new acquisitions and backlogs
  • Maintain accession records and documentation
  • Support stewardship of physical and digital archival materials
  • Collaborate with library and collections staff

Special Collections Archivist Librarian: Denver Public Library

This role involves arranging and describing archival materials across multiple formats, supporting preservation workflows, and providing reference, access, and outreach services.

Responsibilities include:

  • Arrange and describe archival materials in multiple formats
  • Support collections management and preservation activities
  • Provide reference, access, and outreach services
  • Apply archival standards within a public library setting
