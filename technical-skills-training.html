Technical Skill Snapshot

  • Collections Management: Supporting inventory, care, and basic stewardship of physical and digital collection materials.
  • Archival Processing: Working with arrangement, description, and finding aids to support access to archival collections.
  • Digitization: Understanding how digitization and item selection contribute to access, even when not leading projects.
  • Metadata: Using descriptive information to support discovery and use.
  • Visitor Services: Connecting technical work to patron access, reference, and reading-room service.

Skills Identified as Growing in Importance

  • Digital Asset Management Systems (DAMS): Familiarity with digital collections workflows that connect assets, metadata, and access platforms.
  • Coding & Scripting: Introductory skills (e.g., Python) are increasingly common, though not universally required.
  • Community Archives Practices: Supporting community-based collections and ethical access.
